Loomwright · Roadmap v2 · April 2026

From tool
to co-author.

Everything the Atlas proved — that one deep, focused tool beats ten scattered menus — applied to the rest of the app. This round is about making Loomwright anticipate. You have a wild idea; it lands in the right place. You open the app; it tells you what needs attention. You scroll a chapter back; you see exactly what each character was carrying, thinking, wearing, knowing.

The big new feature

Canon Weaver — mass integration engine

You have an idea: "there should be a cursed dagger." You type it once. Canon Weaver reads every chapter, every character, every location — and proposes where it belongs. Added to inventory of Mira (ch.6 Westmark raid). Wielded in ch.14. Referenced in ch.2 as the thing Tobyn warns about. Mentioned in wiki for House Maelgwyn. You accept, edit, or reject each suggestion. One click, the whole canon stays coherent.

Enhancements — every feature, at least two

Every existing workspace earns upgrades from this pass. Numbered so we can pick which ship first.

Home

  • Proactive brief panelThree AI noticings front-and-centre — a dangling thread, a timeline slip, a promising plant. Each with a one-click "take me there to fix it".
  • Daily spark — with the editor's reasoning TomA single AI-generated what-if tuned to your book's current state. Each spark opens into a full editor's note: why the Loom suggests it (e.g. "Edrick's jealousy has no on-page fuel before ch.16 — this would plant it without telegraphing"), what it touches (threads, characters, chapters), and an input box for your counter-vision. The AI weaves your intent back in before producing the draft. Actions: Use this / File under ideas / Not today.
  • Ritual-aware homeLearns your rhythm. Morning: "open yesterday's chapter". Evening on mobile: "capture voice notes?". Weekend: "world-build session?".

Write (Writer's Room)

  • Voice Studio — tunable voice-match TomLive ambient score of "does this sound like you?" trained on your best chapters. Plus a Voice Studio panel: sliders for Formality, Rhythm, Sentence length, Lyricism, Darkness, POV distance, Vocab modernity. Named voice profiles ("Book 1 Mira", "Edrick's POV", "Grimdark mode"), with per-chapter assignment. Every change is versioned — undo, A/B two profiles against the same paragraph, or restore any previous voice.
  • Language workbench — grammar, spelling, rewrite TomInline grammar + spell-check that respects your invented words (auto-learn from the wiki). Select any sentence for: Shorten / Lengthen / Tighten / Flip active↔passive / Vary rhythm / Change register. Right-click any word for thesaurus inline, with "synonyms that match your voice" sorted first. Works on free writing and AI output identically.
  • Sprint timer + session goals15/25/45-min sprints with word-count targets. Silent streaks — never a popup.
  • Version sliderTime-scrub the chapter. Diff any two revisions. Restore any past version instantly.
  • Focus mode + typewriter scrollEverything fades. The current line sits at eye-line. Music suggestions per scene tone.
  • AI chapter co-pilot (boldness 10)Ask for a scene draft. It knows your voice, cast, world, and current chapter context. You keep, edit, or throw away — always in a scratchpad, never auto-merged.

Cast

  • Interview mode — with prompt deck, group chats & writer-handoff TomChat with a character in their voice, drawing on their stats/skills/items/personality/arc-so-far. A rotating prompt deck suggests tuned questions: "Where do you see your story going?", "What item would you most want to find?", "After ch.13 — what would you do next?". Group chats let two or more characters talk to each other (and to you). Every line can be starred, selected, or combined with your own notes — then sent to the AI story writer as a seed for the next chapter. One-click "Turn this into a scene / a plot thread / a new item".
  • Inventory-Over-Chapters (Diablo-style)The new per-character chapter-scrubbable viewer. Items gained, skills learned, stats, wounds, knowledge — at every chapter of the book. Scrub ← → through their journey.
  • Dialogue-by-character extractEvery line this character ever says, all on one page. Edit for tone; changes propagate.
  • Relationship timelineWhen these two last spoke / fought / loved / died for each other — a per-pair mini-timeline.

World

  • Backlinks + "what references this?"Every entry shows every chapter, scene, or character that mentions it. Click to jump.
  • Lore consistency auditor"Harrow is 3 days east in ch.4 but 5 days east in ch.11." Flagged quietly, one-click reconcile.
  • Faction power diagramWho controls what, and how that shifts per chapter. Auto-drawn from your wiki.
  • Artefact provenance chainsFor every object that matters: origin → owners → current location across chapters.

Plot & Timeline

  • Plant-payoff trackerMark a line as a "plant". The app watches for its "payoff" and warns if none is coming. Works the other way too — spot a payoff, it asks "where's the plant?".
  • Dangling-thread alertsOpen threads that haven't been touched for N chapters get a quiet nudge. "Lord Maelgwyn's bargain hasn't moved since ch.7 — intentional?"
  • Thread-density heatmapPer-chapter visualisation of how many threads are active. Spot saggy middles and frantic pile-ups.
  • Parallel-POV aligner"At this moment in the book, where is everyone and what do they know?" A slice-through-time view.

Atlas

  • Biome / terrain brushes + AI auto-generation TomPaint forest, mountain, moor, or let the AI propose a full biome layout from your lore ("windswept northern moors, two rivers, a coastline with three natural harbours") with one click to accept or edit. Works at world, region, and city level.
  • AI-generated floorplans & city maps Tom"Draw me Caer Drun’s great hall" or "give me Prydhaven’s merchant quarter". AI proposes; you nudge walls, rooms, plazas. All floorplans link back to scenes set there.
  • Travel-time calculator"How long from Olm to Caer Drun on horseback?" Uses terrain, roads, weather. Warns if a chapter makes the journey feel wrong.
  • Fog of warOnly show what the POV character has seen so far. A reader-mode atlas.
  • City / region zoomDrill into a city map. Drill into a single tavern floorplan.

Skills / Stats / Items

  • Chapter-linked progressionEvery stat or skill change is pinned to the chapter where it happened. Powers the Inventory viewer.
  • Power budget sanity-check"Your protagonist became too strong too fast around ch.9." Optional, opt-in.

Series & Canon

  • Multi-book libraryOne project can hold a trilogy or a 10-book series. Shared cast, world, skills. Per-book canon flags.
  • Series timelineThe master timeline spans every book. Characters live, age, die across it.
  • Canon diff tool"What changed between Book 2 and Book 3 drafts for Mira?" A clean diff.
  • Reader companion site exportA spoiler-safe web version of your wiki + map for your readers.

Mobile — full companion

  • Write, capture, dictate on phoneFull editor. Voice dictation with inline AI cleanup. Swipe between chapters.
  • Idea capture as first-classA single button: "I had an idea." Shove any fragment in. Canon Weaver sorts it into the right place later.
  • Read mode for reviewingNice typography. Margin AI notes collapsed to icons. One-tap to jump back to desktop writing.

What to build next round

01
Canon Weaver — the mass-integration flow. The flagship. Drop an idea; watch it propose edits across chapters, cast, world, plot, and atlas. Accept/edit/reject each. The biggest new feature.
02
Inventory-Over-Chapters viewer. Diablo II-style per-character progression. Scrub chapter by chapter — items, skills, stats, wounds, relationships, knowledge gained. Includes the inventory grid, skill tree, stat panel, and acquisition log.
03
AI Suggestions Brief on Home + per-tab. The ambient co-author. A home-screen "today's noticings" panel. Each tab has its own "What the Loom saw" sub-panel. Accept/jump-to/dismiss.
04
Mobile Loomwright. Three phone screens: Home (today's thread + capture), Write (dictate + edit), Capture (the one-button idea grab that Canon Weaver then routes).
