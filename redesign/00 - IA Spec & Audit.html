01 / THE PROBLEM

28 doors, no map.

The current app has the right ideas — character snapshots, AI-watched chapters, a deep RPG-style world model — but it ships them as 28 sibling tabs in a five-deep nav. Three of those tabs are timelines. Three are mind-maps. Two route to the same component. Six pieces of code are imported but never rendered.

The fix is not "more nav." It's a smaller set of workspaces that each feel like a complete app, with the underlying tools consolidated, the half-wired things finished or removed, and a single entry point — the Home dashboard — that knows what you're working on right now.

"Make each part feel like its own app, so whichever part a user works on they feel they have full control over their world — and let the AI enhance their vision and track it automatically."
02 / THE REBRAND

Loomwright.

"Claimwise" was a leak from another project. "Grimguff" reads as RPG-only and undersells the writing side. We need a name that says craft + weaving narrative threads + made by you, watched by an intelligence.

Loom·
wright
An omniscient
writer's room
What it means
Loom — the device on which threads are woven into something whole. Plot threads. Character threads. World threads.
Wright — a maker. Playwright, shipwright, wheelwright. You are the maker; the loom is your instrument.
Why it works
Earned, craft-forward, ungeneric. Doesn't say "AI" or "writing tool" anywhere — but the metaphor is exactly the product: many threads, woven by a maker, on a loom that remembers every pass of the shuttle.
Voice
Quiet, precise, slightly archaic. "Save chapter" becomes "weave in." "Consistency check" becomes "follow the thread." Used sparingly; the UI is plain English first, flavor second.
Wordmark
Fraunces (display serif) — set tight, with a low middle dot or hairline rule between "Loom" and "wright." A monogram L·W for favicons and the avatar in the corner.

03 / WHAT'S IN THE BOX

The audit.

Every nav item, every imported component, classified. Three verdicts: keep, merge, kill, plus finish for things that are partially built but worth completing.

Top-level nav, today

Creation (5 items) all keep

  • Personnel · Item Vault · Inventory · Skill Bank · Stat Registry

Five distinct things, all wired. Skill Bank and Skill Tree Visual become a single Skills screen with Library / Tree modes.

Writing (4 items) consolidate

  • Writer's Room · Series Bible · Manuscript Intelligence · Speed Reader

Speed Reader demoted to a button inside Writer's Room and Bible. Manuscript Intelligence moves into the Bible's chapter-detail view (it's how you ingest chapters; it's not a destination).

Visualization (6 items) collapse heavily

  • Plot Timeline · Story Mind Map · UK Map · Master Timeline · Character Arcs · Skill Tree Visual

Three timelines collapse to one. UK Map evolves into Atlas. Skill Tree merges into Skills. Mind Map merges into World Atlas.

Analysis (6 items) collapse heavily

  • Relationships · Plot & Quests · World/Lore · Story Analysis · Wiki Manager · Story Map

Three world-views (Mind Map, Story Map, Wiki) merge into World Atlas. Plot & Quests + Story Analysis (Plot Threads tab) become Plot Lab. Relationships becomes a deep-link from a character — not a top-level nav.

Tools (6 items) demote / move to Settings

  • Story Setup · Search · Version Control · Backup · Sync · Settings

Search becomes the global Cmd+K palette (no nav slot). Version Control, Backup, Sync consolidate into a single Data & History page inside Settings. Story Setup stays accessible from Settings. Settings stays.

Other smells

  • Two component folders. The repo has /components and /src/components; some imports go to one, some to the other. Consolidate to a single tree before redesign work lands.
  • Duplicate routing. activeTab === 'plotthreads' is handled twice in App.js — once in its own block, once in a combined 'plotthreads' || 'consistency' block. Both render StoryAnalysisHub. Means clicking it mounts the component twice.
  • Personnel sub-tabs duplicate top-level nav. Inside a character: Timeline, Relationships, Dialogue, Arc, Stats, Inventory. Externally: Master Timeline, Relationships, Character Arcs. Same data, two doorways. Solution: keep the rich character page; let the top-level views filter to that character via deep link instead of being separate tabs.
  • Keyboard shortcuts drift. Nav shows Alt+1–9; App.js binds Ctrl+1–6. Pick one and align.
04 / THE NEW SHAPE

Six workspaces, one palette.

Instead of 28 siblings, six workspaces that each behave like a complete app. A persistent Cmd+K palette is how you jump anywhere; the sidebar is for orientation, not for finding things.

05 / THE SIX WORKSPACES

What each one is for.

Each workspace is its own focused environment. You can finish a session in just one of them. The AI dock appears in all, contextual to whatever you're touching.

00 / HOME
The dashboard
Where you land. Resume above the fold; story health below.

Top half: your active book + active chapter, last-saved word count, daily goal ring, "continue writing" button. Bottom half: open plot threads, unresolved inconsistencies, characters that haven't appeared in N chapters, what the AI noticed since you last visited.

Replaces: ChapterContextView. Adds: story-health surface (currently buried inside Story Analysis).
01 / WRITE
Writer's Room
The chapter editor + book/chapter tree. The thing you're here to do.
Merges: Writer's Room + Series Bible + Manuscript Intelligence (as the import flow on a chapter). Demotes: Speed Reader → "Read mode" toggle in toolbar.
02 / CAST
Personnel
Characters and their lives across the manuscript.
Keeps: the deep tabbed character page. Removes redundancy: Top-level "Relationships" and "Character Arcs" become deep links into this workspace, filtered.
03 / WORLD
World & Systems
Everything that isn't a person. Items, skills, stats, lore, the Atlas.
Merges: Item Vault + Inventory + Skill Bank + Skill Tree + Stat Registry + World/Lore + Wiki Manager + Story Mind Map. Surfaces: List · Graph · Atlas. Same data, three ways to see it.
04 / PLOT
Plot Lab
Threads, quests, beats, consistency. The story-structure workbench.
Merges: Plot & Quests + Story Analysis + Story Map. One canvas, toolbar of modes: Threads · Beats · Consistency · Structure.
05 / TIMELINE
Chronology
Everything that ever happened, on one ribbon.
Merges: Plot Timeline + Master Timeline + Character Arcs. Filters: Character · Thread · Location · Event type · Book.
06 / ATLAS
The Atlas — UK Map, completed
Real-world places, custom-drawn fantasy realms, hybrid worlds. Auto-pinned from your text. Character paths over time.

Three modes. Globe: Drop-pin on a real-world map; locations mentioned in chapters auto-suggest pins. Maker: Upload or draw a fantasy map; place named pins; tag pins to chapters. Hybrid: Both at once — your character can flee London and arrive in Aetheria. Time scrubber animates character movement across chapters.

Lives in: World workspace, but big enough to deserve its own design doc (06).
06 / DECISION LOG

What I'm proposing, in plain English.

  1. Rebrand to Loomwright. Project, app shell, manifest, capacitor config, README. Three alt names parked.
  2. Collapse 28 nav items to 6 workspaces + Home. Six workspaces that each feel like a complete app.
  3. Cmd+K palette is how you jump. The sidebar is for orientation, not finding things. Full-text + entity + action search.
  4. Six dead imports deleted (WritingCanvas, basic RelationshipTracker, SkillTreeVisualizer, PlotThreadTracker, ConsistencyChecker, GravityMindMap, PlotBeatTracker).
  5. Three timelines → one Timeline with filters. Plot Timeline + Master Timeline + Character Arcs.
  6. Three world-views → World workspace with List / Graph / Atlas surfaces. Wiki + Mind Map + Story Map.
  7. Skills unified. Skill Bank (library) + Skill Tree Visual (graph) become one screen with two modes.
  8. Plot & Quests + Story Analysis → Plot Lab. One canvas, mode toolbar.
  9. Backup + Sync + Version Control → Data & History inside Settings.
  10. Speed Reader demoted. A "Read" toggle in Writer's Room and Bible.
  11. Relationships ceases to be top-level nav. It's the right tab on a character page; it's also a filter on the World graph.
  12. UK Map → Atlas. Real globe + custom map maker + hybrid + auto-pin + character travel timeline.
  13. New: Tutorial Wizard. Spotlight-style walkthrough of every workspace, dismissible, resumable, separate from Story Setup.
  14. AI is the front door. A persistent right-edge dock (collapsible) that knows what you're looking at and offers contextual actions. Replaces today's modal-heavy AI affordances.
  15. Personnel sub-tabs stay rich. They're the source of truth for a character; top-level views deep-link into them rather than replicating.
